# Egypt-Data-Builders-Bootcamp-HR-Data-Analytics-project-Power BI.
This a project i made in the Egypt Data Builders Bootcamp to prove my Knowledge and my Understanding to the track and tools explained. 

**The Goal:**
This Power BI dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of workforce demographics, performance, and payroll insights. The goal is to support data-driven HR decision-making by identifying trends, risks, and opportunities.

**About Dataset:**
It consists of two files (Emplyees , Salary Details): 

**File 1: Employees** 

It include the following columns: 

**employee id, Name, Gender, Age, birth date, department, job title, Hire date, termination date** 
**employment status (active , terminated), Manager Name, location, performance rating, Training hours, Sick days, overtime hours**

**File 2: Salary details**

It include the following columns: 

**employee id, Base Salary, overtime pay, deductions, gross salary, net salary**

**1. Payroll Costs**

Total Payroll Cost: 664K

Average Salary: 2.58K

Overtime Cost %: 28% (very high)

Insight:
A 28% overtime cost ratio is a strong warning sign of:

  - Employee workload imbalance

  - Poor resource or shift planning

  - Potential burnout

Reducing overtime can significantly decrease payroll spending.

**2. Payroll by Department**

Highest payroll costs: Finance (170K), IT (150K), HR (129k) ,Sales (122K)

Lowest: Marketing (93K)

Insight:
Finance and IT consume the largest share of payroll expenses due to higher headcount and specialized roles.
Cost optimization may require reviewing role structures or productivity ratios.

**3. Performance Rating by Department**

Overall Average Performance Rating: 3.08

Top-performing departments: Sales (3.1), HR (3.1), IT (3.1) and Marketing (3.1)

Lower-performing department: Finance (3.0)

Insight:
Departments with higher workloads (like Finance) show slightly lower performance ratings, possibly tied to higher overtime hours.

**4. Average Salary by Department**

Highest salaries in: Finance, sales, HR and Marketing

Lowest salaries in: IT

Insight:
Salary imbalances may influence talent retention and motivation. High performers in low-salary departments may need retention plans.

**5. Overtime Hours by Department**

IT has the highest overtime (1008 hours)

Finance follows with 938 hours

Insight: These two departments are under heavy workload pressure. This explains lower performance ratings and highlights burnout risk.
